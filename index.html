Welcome to the Cybertruck Gen 1 Era

When Tesla finally began delivering Cybertruck units in November 2023, it ended one of the most anticipated vehicle launches in automotive history. Reservation holders who had waited years finally got their hands on a truck that looked nothing like anything else on the road — angular stainless steel panels, a bed vault that opens with a voice command or swipe of the app, and three electric motors producing a claimed 845 horsepower in the Cyberbeast trim.

The Cybertruck Gen 1 is defined by a few core characteristics that every accessory buyer needs to understand: stainless steel exterior panels that cannot be conventionally painted, an air suspension system with adjustable ride height, a powertrain that is fundamentally different from any truck that came before it, and a massive interior dominated by one of the largest center touchscreens in any production vehicle.

This guide covers every major accessory category — what to buy first, what to buy later, and what to skip entirely. Whether you've owned your Cybertruck for three weeks or three years, this is the reference you'll come back to.

Priority Accessories: What to Get First

New Cybertruck owners should focus on protection first. The interior is expensive to repair and the exterior, while tough, has its own vulnerabilities. Here's the recommended acquisition order for first-year Gen 1 owners:

  1. Seat covers — The interior upholstery is one of the most expensive replacement items. Protect it immediately.
  2. Floor mats — All-weather rubber mats prevent sand, mud, and liquids from reaching the factory carpet.
  3. Front protection film (PPF) — The front stainless panels take stone chip damage from highway driving. Clear PPF is invisible and protective.
  4. Bed liner or mat — The stainless bed floor scratches easily from cargo. A mat or spray-in liner is essential.
  5. Frunk and sub-trunk organizers — Immediately improve daily utility from day one.

Seat Covers: The Essential Starting Point

The Cybertruck's seats are comfortable, well-bolstered, and expensive to replace. A quality seat cover set — either neoprene for maximum protection or leatherette for maximum aesthetics — preserves the factory upholstery for years. This is the single most recommended first accessory among the Cybertruck owner community, and for good reason: the interior is where you spend 100% of your time in the vehicle.

For Gen 1, look for high-back bucket seat covers that accommodate the integrated headrest design. Ensure airbag-compatible seams on all front seat covers. A complete set (front pair plus rear bench) typically runs $80–$200 depending on material.

Exterior Accessories

Paint Protection Film (PPF) for Stainless Steel

Despite being stainless steel, the Cybertruck's panels accumulate micro-scratches from road debris, automatic car washes, and daily use. Clear PPF designed for stainless steel surfaces protects the finish without altering the appearance. Self-healing PPF variants repair minor scratches when exposed to heat. Focus on the front hood edge, door edges, and lower rocker panels where stone chips are most frequent.

Bed Liner: Protecting the Stainless Bed Floor

The Cybertruck stainless bed floor scratches readily from cargo, tools, and equipment. Drop-in rubber bed liners or custom-fit mat systems protect the surface from abrasion while providing grip that prevents cargo from sliding. For owners who use the truck for work, a spray-in liner provides the most comprehensive and permanent protection for the bed surface.

The Range Extender: For Long Hauls

One of the most significant capability accessories Tesla offers for the Cybertruck is the optional range extender — an additional battery pack that sits in the truck bed and integrates with the vehicle's power management system. While this is a Tesla-first-party product, it represents the most dramatic single capability improvement available for Gen 1 owners who regularly tow or travel long distances between chargers.

For high-mileage users and frequent tower, the range extender addresses the primary practical limitation of the Gen 1 Cybertruck. Combined with careful route planning using the integrated navigation system's charger routing, it enables road trips and towing missions that would otherwise require careful logistics.

Technology and Connectivity Accessories

The Cybertruck's native technology suite is remarkable, but aftermarket accessories can extend it further. Key technology additions worth considering for Gen 1 owners:

  • Wireless charging pads: Secondary charging surfaces beyond the factory wireless charger for front passenger use
  • USB hub expansions: The Cybertruck's USB-C ports are numerous but distributing them with a hub adds flexibility
  • Dash cam integration: While the Cybertruck has built-in Sentry Mode cameras, dedicated dashcams provide higher resolution front video
  • Backup camera enhancement: Third-party camera systems that interface with the display can improve coverage angles

Frequently Asked Questions

When did Cybertruck Gen 1 deliveries start?

Tesla began official Cybertruck deliveries in November 2023 at a special event at Gigafactory Texas. Customer deliveries ramped through 2024 and continued expanding into 2025 and 2026.

What are the trim levels available for Cybertruck Gen 1?

Cybertruck Gen 1 launched with two main configurations: the dual-motor All-Wheel Drive (Foundation Series) and the tri-motor Cyberbeast. A single-motor Rear-Wheel Drive configuration was announced for later availability.

Is the Cybertruck body stainless steel?

Yes. The Cybertruck uses 30X cold-rolled stainless steel body panels. The material cannot be painted conventionally and develops a brushed patina over time. It's extremely resistant to dents and corrosion compared to conventional steel or aluminum panels.

What accessories does Tesla offer for the Cybertruck?

Tesla offers first-party accessories including the camp kitchen attachment, range extender battery, ATV charging ramp, and various interior and exterior accessories through the Tesla Shop online and at service centers.

What is the Cybertruck's ground clearance?

The Cybertruck with air suspension can achieve approximately 17 inches of ground clearance in its highest setting, making it one of the most capable off-road trucks at factory stock configuration.

Can you charge other devices from the Cybertruck?

Yes. The Cybertruck has Vehicle-to-Load (V2L) capability, allowing it to power external devices through its 120V and 240V outlets. It can power tools, appliances, and even homes during power outages.

What is the Cybertruck's range?

EPA-rated range varies by configuration: dual-motor AWD is rated around 318 miles, and the Cyberbeast tri-motor is rated around 320 miles. Adding the range extender battery option significantly increases this range.

Are Cybertruck accessories compatible across all trims?

Most interior accessories are compatible across trims since the cabin is largely shared between configurations. Exterior accessories may vary — always confirm fitment for your specific trim when purchasing.

How do I care for the stainless steel exterior?

Use stainless steel-specific cleaners and microfiber cloths following the direction of the grain. Avoid abrasive scrubbers. PPF (paint protection film) can protect the surface without changing the appearance significantly.

What is the best first accessory to buy for a new Cybertruck?

Most owners prioritize seat covers first to protect the interior, followed by a bed liner or cargo organizer, and then exterior protection film for the front panels most exposed to road debris and stone chips.
